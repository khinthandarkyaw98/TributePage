Jae and Years

  • 1992 - Born in Buenos Aires, Argentina
  • 2012 - took a leave from studying political science at California State University, Long Beach
  • 2015 - debuted as a vocalist and electric guitarist of JYP's first rock band, Day6
  • 2016 - became an MC of Arirang's After School Club
  • 2017 - started a Youtube channel called JaeSix (later renamed eaJ)
  • 2018 - quit from Arirang's After School Club and joined as a guest DJ every Monday on Arirang Radio's Monday Music Access with singer Bernard Park
  • 2020 - started his first podcast "How Did I Get Here?" (HDIGH) with Dive Studios. On May 10, 2020, JYP Entertainment announced that DAY6's would be taking a hiatus due to mental health concerns of some members. AleXa joined his podcast as a co-host in early August. HDIGH Podcast won "People's Choice" and "Entertainment" Category by Podcast Award in 2020 and Best Podcast 2020 by Apple Podcast. On September 15, 2020, he initiated From Friends, a project partnered with Represent, selling clothing and other merchandise.He donated $100,000 of the proceeds to the Jed Foundation, which helps provide free mental health resources to teenagers and young adults. Jae started streaming himself and friends playing video games, such as Among Us, Animal Crossing, Crossy Road, Roblox, and Valorant. On September 21, 2020, Jae, alongside 2PM's Nichkhun, took on roles as members of crime-fighting boyband "4 2 Sing" in season three of Big Hero 6: The Series. Jae played twins Kwang-Sun and Ye Joon, one half of the boy band "4 2 Sing".On November 27, 2020, DPR Live collaborated with Crush and Jae for “Jam & Butterfly”.
  • 2021 - stopped streaming on Twitch. Jae and his band released 'The Book of Us : Negentropy" in April. Jae performed in the online concert (Asia Rising Together) arranged by 88rising.
